UPON hearing the counsel the court made the following

O R D E R

1. Heard learned counsel for the parties.

2. The present petition challenges the order dated 15.02.2024 passed
by the High Court of Delhi whereby the High Court directed release of
the vehicle seized under Section 482 CrPC.

Facts of the Case:
The respondent company was transporting goods when intercepted by 
police on 10.10.2023. Vehicle bearing No. DL 01 AB 1234 contained 
30 cartons of counterfeit medicines violating Drugs and Cosmetics Act,
1940. FIR No. 567/2023 registered u/s 420, 485 IPC and Sec. 18 AC
D&C Act.

Issues:
(i) Whether High Court erred in directing release without trial conclusion?
(ii) Whether Section 457 CrPC applicable to vehicles seized u/s 482?

Arguments by Petitioner:
Learned counsel for State submitted that vehicle integral to crime,
release would hamper investigation. Cited State of Maharashtra vs.
Pratapsing 2002 AIR 526 where SLPs admitted against such releases.

Learned counsel relied on Section 451 CrPC requiring 'sufficient cause'.
No such cause made out as trial pending and vehicle evidence.

Arguments by Respondent:
Mr. Mehta contended vehicle commercial asset, owner innocent third
party. Driver absconded but vehicle depreciation continuing. Relied
on SCC 2019 (7) 456 where similar release ordered pending trial.

Held:
We find High Court order suffers from non-application of mind to 
provisions of CrPC. Vehicle used in smuggling cannot be released 
mechanically. SLP admitted. High Court order stayed. Matter listed
after six weeks.
